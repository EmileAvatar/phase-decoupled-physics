## Theory-by-Theory Breakdown

### Newton's Gravity (1687)

**What it says:** Every mass attracts every other mass with force
F = GMm/r². Gravity is instant. Space and time are absolute.

**What it gets right:**
- Planetary orbits (to very high accuracy)
- Tides, trajectories, rocket science
- Everything you encounter in daily life

**What it gets wrong:**
- Mercury's orbit precesses slightly more than Newton predicts
- Light bends twice as much as Newton would predict
- GPS clocks tick at different rates (time is not absolute)
- Doesn't explain *why* masses attract

**Where it sits in PDTP:**

Newton is the **weak-field, slow-speed limit** of PDTP.

```
PDTP field equation:  □φ = Σ gᵢ sin(ψᵢ − φ)
                              ↓
                      weak field: sin(x) ≈ x
                      static: □ → ∇²
                      slow speed: no relativistic corrections
                              ↓
Newton's equation:    ∇²Φ = 4πGρ
                              ↓
Newton's force:       F = −GMm/r²
```

Every equation Newton wrote still works. PDTP just explains *why* it works
(phase-locking) and adds corrections that only matter for extreme objects
like neutron stars and black holes.

**Simple analogy:** Newton is like knowing that dropping a ball makes it
fall. PDTP is like knowing that the ball falls because its matter-waves
synchronize with spacetime's waves. The ball still falls either way.

### Einstein's General Relativity (1915)

**What it says:** Mass-energy curves spacetime. Objects move along the
straightest possible paths (geodesics) in curved spacetime. Gravity is
not a force — it's geometry.

**What it gets right:**
- Everything Newton gets right, plus:
- Mercury's orbital precession (exactly)
- Gravitational lensing (light bending, exactly)
- Gravitational time dilation (GPS depends on this)
- Gravitational waves (detected by LIGO 2015)
- Black holes (imaged by Event Horizon Telescope 2019)

**What it doesn't explain:**
- *Why* mass curves spacetime (it just says it does)
- What the gravitational constant G actually is (it's measured, not derived)
- How gravity connects to quantum mechanics
- Whether gravity can be modified or controlled

**Where it sits in PDTP:**

Einstein's equations are the **macroscopic average** of phase-locking
dynamics — proved in Part 12 (tetrad extension).

```
PDTP (microscopic level):
  - Spacetime is a condensate with phase φ and tetrad structure e^a_μ
  - Order parameter: Φ = √ρ₀ e^{iφ} e^a_μ
  - Matter-waves ψ couple via cos(ψ − φ)

        ↓ average over many phase-locked waves
        ↓ (like averaging water molecules → fluid dynamics)

Einstein (macroscopic level):
  - Spacetime is a smooth manifold with metric g_μν
  - Einstein's equation: G_μν = 8πG T_μν
  - Matter follows geodesics
```

The relationship is like **fluid dynamics and molecules:**

| Fluid Dynamics | Molecules |
|---------------|-----------|
| Smooth flow, pressure, density | Individual atoms bouncing around |
| Navier-Stokes equations | Newton's laws for each atom |
| Works perfectly at large scales | Needed to explain *why* fluids work |
| Can't explain surface tension from first principles | Explains surface tension naturally |

| Einstein's GR | PDTP |
|--------------|------|
| Smooth curved spacetime | Individual phase-locked waves |
| Einstein's field equations | PDTP field equations for φ and ψ |
| Works perfectly for all observations | Explains *why* mass curves spacetime |
| Can't explain what G is | Could derive G from condensate density |

**Simple analogy:** Einstein is like knowing the rules of a traffic
pattern — cars follow lanes and curves. PDTP is like knowing *why* the
traffic pattern exists — because each driver (matter-wave) is
synchronized with the road system (spacetime phase).

### Quantum Mechanics (1925)

**What it says:** At small scales, particles are waves. Physics is
probabilistic. You can't know position and momentum simultaneously
(uncertainty principle).

**What it gets right:**
- All of chemistry
- All of electronics (semiconductors, lasers, etc.)
- Nuclear physics
- Every particle physics experiment ever done

**Where it sits in PDTP:**

Quantum mechanics is **not replaced by PDTP.** It is an **ingredient.**

PDTP *uses* quantum mechanics:
- The matter phase ψ IS a quantum mechanical wavefunction
- de Broglie's relation (λ = h/p) is assumed, not derived
- The wave nature of matter is the starting point, not the conclusion

What PDTP adds to QM:
- A mechanism for gravity (QM alone can't explain gravity)
- A connection between quantum phase and spacetime geometry
- The possibility that spacetime itself is quantum (a condensate)

**Simple analogy:** QM is like knowing that every person has a heartbeat.
PDTP says: "Yes, and when many heartbeats synchronize with the rhythm
of the ground they're standing on, that synchronization IS gravity."
PDTP doesn't change heartbeats — it uses them.

### Quantum Field Theory / Standard Model (1970s)

**What it says:** Forces arise from exchanging virtual particles.
Electromagnetism = photon exchange. Strong force = gluon exchange.
Weak force = W/Z boson exchange.

**Where it sits in PDTP:**

The Standard Model is **unchanged** by PDTP. PDTP only addresses gravity —
the one force the Standard Model can't handle.

PDTP adds one new field (spacetime phase φ) alongside the Standard Model's
fields. It doesn't modify how electrons, quarks, or photons behave.

The dream: eventually unify the Standard Model forces with PDTP's
gravitational phase-locking. But this is future work, not current PDTP.

  semiconductors, lasers, and MRI machines

## U(1) vs SU(2) vs SU(3) — Why PDTP Needs All Three

PDTP uses three symmetry groups. They are not separate theories — they are
layers of the **same** theory, each capturing more physics than the last.

### What Are These Groups? (Plain Language)

**U(1)** = rotations around a circle. The field phi is a single angle
(one number at each point in space). Think of a clock hand that can only
point in one direction at a time.

**SU(2)** = rotations of a 2x2 matrix. The field has 3 internal directions
(the Pauli matrices). Think of 3 clock hands — enough to describe spin
and the weak nuclear force.

**SU(3)** = rotations of a 3x3 matrix. The field U has 8 internal directions
(the Gell-Mann matrices). Think of 8 clock hands — enough to describe
the shape of 3D space and the strong nuclear force.

### How They Relate

```
SU(3)  [8 DOF — full theory]
  |
  ├── contains SU(2) [3 DOF — weak force, spin]
  |
  └── contains U(1) [1 DOF — simplified gravity]
```

The U(1) Lagrangian is the **special case** of the SU(3) Lagrangian when
you shrink the 3x3 matrix to a single number:

- SU(3) coupling: Re[Tr(Psi^dag U)] / 3
- U(1) limit: Re[Tr(Psi^dag U)] / 1 = cos(psi - phi)

This was proved in Part 37. They are **the same equation** at different
levels of detail.

### What Each Group Handles in PDTP

| Feature | U(1) | SU(2) | SU(3) |
|---------|------|-------|-------|
| **DOF per point** | 1 | 3 | 8 |
| **Field** | angle phi | 2x2 matrix | 3x3 matrix U |
| **Coupling** | cos(psi-phi) | Tr(Psi^dag U)/2 | Re[Tr(Psi^dag U)]/3 |
| **Time curvature (g_00)** | YES | YES | YES |
| **Spatial curvature (g_ij)** | NO (gamma=0) | NO (only 3 DOF, need 6) | YES (8 DOF > 6) |
| **Light deflection** | 0.875" (half GR) | -- | 1.75" (matches GR) |
| **PPN gamma** | 0 (ruled out) | -- | 1 (correct) |
| **Gravitational waves** | scalar (breathing) | -- | tensor (h_+, h_x) |
| **Strong force (QCD)** | -- | -- | 8 gluons, confinement |
| **Weak force** | -- | W+, W-, Z (3 bosons) | -- |
| **Particle types** | -- | Z_2 vortices (fermions) | Z_3 vortices (quarks) |
| **Generations** | -- | -- | 3 (from |Z_3| = 3) |

### Why Not Just Use SU(3) for Everything?

Because SU(3) is mathematically harder. The U(1) version gives correct
answers for many things (Newtonian gravity, Hawking radiation, horizons,
dark energy). We use U(1) when it works and upgrade to SU(3) when needed.

This is exactly like the Newton-to-Einstein relationship:
- Newton (simple) handles 90% of gravity problems
- Einstein (complex) needed for the other 10%
- U(1) (simple) handles time curvature, horizons, Hawking
- SU(3) (complex) handles spatial curvature, lensing, tensor GW, QCD

### Is There a U(2)?

The group SU(2) (not U(2)) has 3 DOF and describes the weak nuclear force
in PDTP. It gives W+, W-, and Z bosons from its 3 generators. SU(2) also
provides Z_2 vortices (winding 1/2) which give fermion statistics and
spin-1/2 (Part 93). But SU(2) is too small to source the spatial metric
(needs 6 DOF, SU(2) only has 3).

### The Full PDTP Lagrangian (One Equation)

All three groups combine into **one** Lagrangian:

```
L_full = K Tr[(d_mu U^dag)(d^mu U)]
       + Sum_i K_i Tr[(d_mu Psi_i^dag)(d^mu Psi_i)]
       + Sum_i g_i Re[Tr(Psi_i^dag U)] / 3
```

- U(x) in SU(3): the spacetime condensate field (8 DOF)
- Psi_i(x) in SU(3): matter fields (8 DOF each)
- The coupling Re[Tr(Psi^dag U)]/3 is the Wilson loop action

When U and Psi are restricted to U(1), this reduces to the familiar:

```
L = (1/2)(d_mu phi)^2 + (1/2)(d_mu psi)^2 + g cos(psi - phi)
```

There is only one theory. U(1) is the simplified version.
