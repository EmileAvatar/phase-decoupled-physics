## Summary: Phase-Coupled Gravity vs Einstein Field Equations

Modern physics faces a core unresolved problem:
**Einstein’s Field Equations (general relativity) are classical**, while **matter is fundamentally quantum**.
Coupling classical spacetime geometry to quantum energy via expectation values works only approximately and fails in extreme regimes (vacuum energy, singularities, early universe).

This suggests the issue is **conceptual, not merely mathematical**.

---

## Core Reframing (PDTP Perspective)

This framework proposes a shift in ontology:

* **Matter** is modeled as stable standing-wave phase fields
* **Spacetime** is modeled as a wave-supporting phase field
* **Gravity** is not a force or particle exchange, but **phase-locking** between matter-wave phases and spacetime phase gradients
* **Inertia** arises from the same phase-coupling mechanism (equivalence principle is natural)

Spacetime does not respond to raw energy density, but to **phase mismatch and synchronization stress**.

## Toy Phase-Coupling Equation

A minimal illustrative (non-rigorous) field model:

* Let `φ(x,t)` be the spacetime phase field
* Let `ψᵢ(x,t)` be matter-wave phase fields

Toy Lagrangian:

```
ℒ = ½(∂μφ)(∂^μφ)
  + Σᵢ ½(∂μψᵢ)(∂^μψᵢ)
  − Σᵢ gᵢ cos(ψᵢ − φ)
```

Resulting spacetime phase equation:

```
□φ = Σᵢ gᵢ sin(ψᵢ − φ)
```

Interpretation:

* Spacetime reacts to **phase mismatch**, not absolute energy
* Classical gravity emerges when phases are nearly locked
* Rapid quantum fluctuations average out and do not strongly gravitate

## Why This Addresses the GR–QM Tension

* Vacuum energy produces large quantum fluctuations but **minimal net phase-lock stress**
* This naturally weakens gravity’s response to quantum vacuum effects
* Gravity and inertia share a single coupling parameter (`g`)
* The equivalence principle emerges without postulates

Einstein’s equations are interpreted as an **emergent, coarse-grained limit** of deeper phase-synchronization dynamics.
