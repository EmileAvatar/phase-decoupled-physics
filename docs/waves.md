## 🌊 What is a SOFAR Channel?

The **SOFAR channel** (Sound Fixing And Ranging) is a **natural underwater waveguide** that lets sound travel **extremely long distances with very little loss**.

* Found in oceans worldwide (typically ~600–1200 m deep)
* Used by submarines, whales, and ocean sensors
* Sound can travel **thousands of kilometers**

---

## ⚙️ Core Mechanism (simple but powerful)

The key idea:

> **Sound speed in water depends on temperature + pressure (depth)**

* Near surface → warm → sound speed **high**
* Mid-depth → colder → sound speed **lower**
* Deep ocean → pressure dominates → speed **increases again**

👉 This creates a **minimum sound speed layer**

---

## 🔽 Sound Speed Profile (the important shape)

* Top: fast
* Middle (SOFAR axis): **slowest**
* Bottom: fast again

This creates a **“wave trap”**

---

## 🌀 What happens to sound?

* If sound goes **upward** → enters faster region → bends **back down**
* If sound goes **downward** → enters faster region → bends **back up**

👉 Result:

* Sound is continuously **refracted back toward the center**
* It **oscillates around the channel axis**
* It **doesn’t escape easily**

---

## 📡 Why it’s special

* Minimal energy loss
* No need for reflection off boundaries
* Acts like a **natural fiber optic cable for sound**
